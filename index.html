Visualizing Graphlets in the Brain

What is this?

This application visualizes research done in the area of brain topology mapping.

Displayed are two topological abstractions of the human brain, each with exactly 200 nodes representing different geographic regions in the brain. The right brain is the control group, while the left brain is the OCD group. Both brains were derived from a resting state.

This tool makes use of the research done by Sridevi Kamla Maharaj and visualizes different graph theoretical aspects of the human brain as seen in OCD and non-OCD children. Namely, the model is concerned with graphlets and the frequency in which nodes take part as a member of an orbit in each graphlet.

How to Use

adjust

You may click on any node to reveal that node's neighbors. Clicking on the same node deselects the node and returns the graph to its original state.

Dragging either graph will move and rotate it.

menu

Open the menu by clicking the menu icon in the upper right hand corner. The menu provides a suite of tools to alter and change the graph's visualization.

space_bar

Tapping the spacebar brings up the quick navigation for coloring the nodes by orbit frequency. Just enter the orbit you would like to see and press enter. Tapping the space bar a second time closes the window

arrow_back arrow_forward

In order to facilitate the quick exploration of orbit frequencies, you may use the back and forward arrow to increment or decrement the orbit id. The orbits range from orbit 0, to orbit 72.
